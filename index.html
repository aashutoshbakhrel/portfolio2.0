About Me

Nothing much to tell.
But I’ll make myself visible, piece by piece — hoping your mind creates a space to understand me, if you care to.

I was born in Bagmati Province, Nepal — just a boy —
but I became a man when consciousness hit me hard, thousands of miles away, in Thunder Bay, Canada.

Academically, I’ve never been disciplined.
But grades never reflected that.
From Grade 8 to Grade 12, I held a GPA above 3.5 — even a 4.0 in 11th and 12th.
By numbers, I wasn’t a failure.
By instinct, I was.

Why?
Because I realized academics didn’t align with my consciousness — with the identity I felt growing inside.

I earned a rank good enough for IOE Pulchowk’s Electrical Engineering.
I secured a full scholarship for Computer Engineering at IOE WRC, Pokhara.
And I walked away.

Then I dropped a $16,000/year scholarship at St. Louis University.
Then a $10,000 CAD scholarship at one of Canada’s top research universities.
I travelled across systems, borders, visa portals and airplanes —
and after a few lunar revolutions, my consciousness knocked again:

“You don’t have time here to build something. Go home.”


So I did.

I returned with nothing but clarity —
and now I’m pursuing a degree that aligns with what I truly want:
Computer Science, or anything close enough to satisfy my consciousness.

Yes, I’ve dropped two valuable bachelor’s degrees.
Not out of carelessness —
but for FU.N: Fulfillment of Needs (of my consciousness).
A strange compass, but it never lies.

As for skills:
my mind loves doing things.
Not one thing — things. Many things.
Maths, problem-solving, dark topics like darkmatter, darkenery (did you catch that joke?),
and building projects that feel like fragments of a bigger mind I want to create one day.

Below are some of the things I’ve built —
pieces of me, visible enough for now.
